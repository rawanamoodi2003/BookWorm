The Number Two Classic #2

Little Women

Louisa May Alcott

4.00
Category: Classic

The story begins with the March family living in genteel poverty in Concord, Massachusetts. The girls' father is serving as a chaplain in the Union Army during the Civil War. The mother, Marmee, is a strong and compassionate woman who guides her daughters with love and wisdom.Each of the four sisters has her own distinct personality. Meg is the eldest and responsible, Jo is the tomboyish and independent aspiring writer, Beth is gentle and musically inclined, and Amy is artistic and somewhat vain.

Language: English

About The
Author

Louisa May Alcott

Louisa May Alcott was a famous American writer who was born in 1832 and died in 1888. She was best known for her famous novel “The Girls’ Club,” which was published in 1868 and became a great success. Alcott was considered a pioneer of writing stories for girls and young adults, and wrote many novels and short stories that focus on the values of friendship, family, and courage. Alcott was considered an icon of American literature, and among her most important works, in addition to “The Girls Club,” are novels such as “The Prince and the Adventurer,” “The Little Palace,” “The Genius,” and others.
