Sherlock Holmes: The Complete
Novels and Stories Volume I

Sir Arthur Conan Doyle

3.5
Category: detective fiction

Bantam's two paperback volumes offer a complete collection of Sherlock Holmes's adventures, including all fifty-six short stories and four novels. Volume I includes the early novel "A Study in Scarlet," introducing Holmes and Watson, along with "The Sign of Four" and famous cases like "The Adventure of the Speckled Band" and "The Five Orange Pips." These tales bring Victorian England to life, showcasing Holmes's unmatched detective skills at 221B Baker Street.

About The
Author

Sir Arthur Conan Doyle

Sir Arthur Ignatius Conan Doyle was a British writer and physician, born on May 22, 1859, and passing away on July 7, 1930. He is best known for creating the character Sherlock Holmes, one of the most famous detectives in literature. Doyle's works also include science fiction stories, historical novels, and plays, but it's his Sherlock Holmes stories that have had the most enduring impact. The adventures of Holmes and his loyal companion Dr. John Watson have captivated readers for generations and remain immensely popular to this day.
