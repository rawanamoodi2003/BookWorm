The Mysterious Case of Sir Claud's Stolen Formula

Sir Claude Amory's formula for a new powerful explosive has been stolen, most likely by a member of his extended family. Sir Claude gathers the suspects in the library and locks the door, telling them that when the lights go out, the formula must be replaced on the table - and no questions will be asked. But when the lights go on, Sir Claude dies. Now Hercule Poirot, with the help of Captain Hastings and Inspector Japp, must unravel a tangle of family feuds, old flames and suspicious aliens to find the killer and prevent a global catastrophe.

About The
Author

Agatha Christie

Agatha Christie, the iconic English writer, is celebrated as the "Queen of Mystery" for her timeless contributions to detective fiction. With memorable characters like Hercule Poirot and Miss Marple, Christie crafted intricate plots and surprising twists that captivated readers worldwide. Her classic novels such as "Murder on the Orient Express" and "And Then There Were None" continue to enthrall audiences, ensuring her status as one of the most influential authors in literary history.
